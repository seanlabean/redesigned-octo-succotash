Clouds and Cloud Structures

Cumulus

Cumulus (from the Latin cumulo- meaning heap or pile) clouds are likely what most people think of when asked to picture a cloud. Indeed, when asked to draw a picture of nature, children aged kindergarten through eighth grade, if they included clouds in their drawing, almost exclusively drew cumulus clouds. They're clumpy, fluffy, low-lying (< 2000m) cotton balls generated via atmospheric convection and can appear alone, in lines, or in clusters. They rarely produce precipitation, but may transition to more dense or chaotic cloud structures.
