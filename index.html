About Me


I am currently a Ph.D. candidate at Drexel University where my research focuses on computational astrophysics and the modeling of star cluster formation. I received my Masters degree in physics from Drexel Univeristy in 2019. Prior to graduate school, I received my B.S. in physics at California Polytechnic State University — San Luis Obispo in 2016.

Teaching

To have learned is to be able to teach. To be able to teach is to understand concepts at a granular level. In my classes, I expect my students to become learners of physics but to become teachers of it too.

I am a teaching assistant at Drexel University. I have been a recitation instructor and grader for the calculus-based introductory physics courses PHYS101: Fundamentals of Physics I, PHYS102: Fundamentals of Physics II, PHYS201: Fundamentals of Physics III, and PHYS121: Physiscs fro Art and Design Students. I was also a grader for PHYS431: Galactic Astrophysics. Since 2020, I've also led the yearly Python-based coding bootcamp for incoming physics graduate students the code for which can be found on my Github.

In 2019, I was honored by the Drexel Physics Department with a Teaching Excelence Award. In 2022 I was nominated to receive the graduate college TA Excelence Award.
